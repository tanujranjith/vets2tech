In times of crisis, the Veteran IT Guide steps in to provide essential support. For young individuals seeking positive opportunities, the guide has a history of mentoring them into responsible citizens. Veterans needing advocacy for careers, education, healthcare, and stable homes receive daily support, free of charge.

Active-duty, National Guard, and Reserve personnel have dedicated allies in the Veteran IT Guide. With a focus on national defense, veterans understand the critical role of home-front support. They remind us that freedom and prosperity come with a cost, often paid in sacrifice. They also differentiate between Memorial Day and Veterans Day, and work tirelessly to create monuments and memorials that honor and preserve the memory of past military service.

The Veteran IT Guide salutes the flag and encourages everyone to respect the unity, freedom, and hope it represents. Within this guide, there is no rank or prejudice—only a unified purpose. The mission is to strengthen the United States of America through time-honored programs, services, compassion, and actions that have made a significant impact for over a century.

Mission Statement

As adopted in October 2020, the Veteran IT Guide’s mission is to enhance the well-being of America's veterans, their families, our military, and our communities through mutual helpfulness. The vision is “Veterans Strengthening America.”

Core Values

  • A VETERAN IS A VETERAN – The Veteran IT Guide embraces all current and former military members, helping them transition smoothly into civilian life.
  • SELFLESS SERVICE – Celebrates those who contribute to causes greater than themselves and inspires others to strengthen America through service.
  • AMERICAN VALUES AND PATRIOTISM – Advocates for the Constitution, equal justice, youth education, and the honoring of military service through memorial events.
  • FAMILY AND COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT – Addresses the unique needs of local communities.
  • ADVANCING THE VISION – Educates, mentors, and leads new generations of Americans.
  • HONORING PAST SACRIFICES – Pays perpetual respect to military sacrifices to ensure they are remembered by future generations.

Motto

The Veteran IT Guide's motto is “Veterans Strengthening America.”

Preamble

FOR GOD AND COUNTRY WE ASSOCIATE OURSELVES TOGETHER FOR THE FOLLOWING PURPOSES:

  • To uphold and defend the Constitution of the United States of America;
  • To maintain law and order;
  • To foster and perpetuate a one hundred percent Americanism;
  • To preserve the memories and incidents of our associations in all wars;
  • To inculcate a sense of individual obligation to the community, state, and nation;
  • To combat the autocracy of both the classes and the masses;
  • To make right the master of might;
  • To promote peace and goodwill on earth;
  • To safeguard and transmit to posterity the principles of justice, freedom, and democracy;
  • To consecrate and sanctify our comradeship by our devotion to mutual helpfulness.
